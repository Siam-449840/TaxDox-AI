     1→# TAXDOX AI — Complete Product Specification & Market Research Document
     2→
     3→## Executive Summary
     4→
     5→TaxDox AI is an end-to-end tax document intelligence platform built for accounting firms, with its core focus on solving the industry's most painful problem: **PBC (Prepared by Client) document management**. There are document processing tools on the market (Magnetic, Black Ore), and there are PBC management tools (Suralink, Byron), but **no product seamlessly integrates the two** — and this is precisely TaxDox AI's differentiating advantage.

     8→## Part One: In-Depth Market Research
     9→
    10→### 1.1 Industry Background: The Accounting Industry's Talent Crisis
    11→
    12→The accounting industry is facing an unprecedented talent shortage. Since 2021, **300,000 accountants have left the industry**. CPA candidate numbers have been declining consistently, and firms cannot find replacement talent. Meanwhile, across the United States, **80 million individual tax returns are filed through professionals each year**, representing a **$2 billion outsourcing market**, with individual CPA firms paying **$100,000 to $1 million annually** for this service.
    13→
    14→> "Accountants don't have a demand problem; they have a capacity problem." — Blaze O'Byrne, Co-founder of Byron
    15→
    16→### 1.2 Core Pain Point: The Chaos of PBC Document Management
    17→
    18→**Real-world case**: The tax team at JDS Professional Group uses a client portal to send and receive files, but the portal **does not allow preparers to organize received information in a structured way**. Clients send files through various channels including email, USB drives, and even CDs. In large-scale business entities where clients send **over 100 documents**, this lack of organization quickly becomes overwhelming.
    19→
    20→> "Clients give us files, but we can't confirm whether this is what we need. It's like a digital shoebox." — Peter Ordelheide, Tax Manager at JDS

    39→### 1.4 Market Gap Analysis
    40→
    41→#### Gap 1: Missing Intelligent PBC Layer
    42→
    43→Existing PBC management tools (Suralink, AuditDashboard) can only **organize and track** document requests but cannot **understand** document content. AI extraction tools (Magnetic, Black Ore) can extract data but lack PBC workflow management.
    44→
    45→**How TaxDox AI fills the gap**: Automatically generate a **data extraction template** for each request item when the PBC request is sent; immediately perform **intelligent validation** upon document upload (checking completeness, correct formatting, and matching the request); and **automatically map** extracted data to corresponding fields in tax software.
    46→
    47→#### Gap 2: Affordability Gap for Small and Mid-sized Firms
    48→
    49→Black Ore serves Top 100 firms with extremely high pricing. Magnetic and Byron are mid-priced but feature-incomplete. Small firms (1-10 accountants) cannot afford expensive end-to-end solutions.
    50→
    51→**How TaxDox AI fills the gap**: Adopts a **pay-as-you-go** model with low starting prices that scale with growth. The MVP can be built using low-code/AI tools within 30-90 days.
    52→
    53→#### Gap 3: Missing Cross-Border Tax Support
    54→
    55→Existing solutions primarily focus on the U.S. tax system. With **over 190 countries** having different tax systems, multinational enterprises and international individuals urgently need a platform capable of processing tax documents from multiple countries.
    56→
    57→**How TaxDox AI fills the gap**: Builds a **configurable tax rules engine** supporting tax system plugins for multiple countries. MVP supports the U.S., UK, Canada, India, and Australia, with expansion capabilities for the future.
    58→
    59→#### Gap 4: Client Experience Gap
    60→
    61→Existing tools require clients to learn complex portals. Poor client experience leads to delayed document submissions.
    62→
    63→**How TaxDox AI fills the gap**: A **zero-learning-curve** client interface — clients simply **drag and drop files or send emails**, and the AI automatically classifies, validates, and matches request items.

   127→### 3.1 Intelligent PBC List Generation Module
   128→
   129→#### Function Description
   130→Automatically generates personalized PBC (Prepared by Client) document request lists for each client.
   131→
   132→#### Detailed Feature Points
   133→
   134→**3.1.1 Intelligent List Generation**
   135→- **Based on prior year data**: Extracts required document types from the previous year's tax returns
   136→- **Client persona-driven**: Generates different templates based on client type (individual/business/trust/nonprofit)
   137→- **Dynamic adjustment**: Automatically updates request items based on current year's tax law changes
   138→- **Multi-language support**: Supports generating request lists in English, Spanish, Chinese, and other languages
   139→
   140→**3.1.2 List Customization**
   141→- **Drag-and-drop editor**: Tax managers can drag to reorder request items
   142→- **Conditional logic**: If client has rental income, automatically add Schedule E related requests
   143→- **Annotation capability**: Add explanations for each request item (e.g., "Please provide all 2025 1099-INT forms")
   144→- **Priority marking**: Flag documents as urgent/important/regular
   145→
   146→**3.1.3 Automated Distribution**
   147→- **One-click send**: Distribute via multiple channels including email, portal, and SMS
   148→- **Personalized links**: Each client receives a unique upload link
   149→- **Bulk operations**: Send annual PBC lists to all clients simultaneously
   150→
   151→### 3.2 Intelligent Document Receipt and Classification Module
   152→
   153→#### Function Description
   154→Automatically receives, classifies, and organizes all uploaded tax documents.
   155→
   156→#### Detailed Feature Points
   157→
   158→**3.2.1 Multi-Channel Receipt**
   159→- **Client portal upload**: Drag-and-drop upload, supports batch processing
   160→- **Email forwarding**: Clients can forward documents to `upload@taxdox.ai`, system automatically associates with corresponding client
   161→- **Mobile photo capture**: Mobile app photo upload, AI automatically optimizes image quality
   162→- **Cloud import**: Import from Box, Google Drive, OneDrive/SharePoint
   163→- **Batch ZIP upload**: Supports uploading entire folder ZIP archives
   164→
   165→**3.2.2 AI Intelligent Classification**
   166→Supports automatic identification of **100+ types of tax documents**:
   167→
   168→| Category | Document Types |
   169→|----------|----------------|
   170→| **Income Documents** | W-2, 1099-NEC, 1099-MISC, 1099-INT, 1099-DIV, 1099-B, 1099-R, K-1(1065/1120S/1041) |
   171→| **Deduction Documents** | Mortgage interest 1098, Property tax bills, Charitable donation receipts, Medical expense receipts, Education expense 1098-T |
   172→| **Identity Documents** | Driver's license, Passport, SSN card, EIN confirmation letter |
   173→| **Business Documents** | Profit and loss statements, Balance sheets, Bank statements, Credit card statements, Payroll reports |
   174→| **Investment Documents** | Brokerage statements, Cryptocurrency transaction records, Dividend reinvestment plans |
   175→| **Real Estate Documents** | Property purchase documents, Rental income records, Depreciation schedules, Property tax assessments |
   176→| **Other** | Alimony agreements, Divorce decrees, Student loan 1098-E, Health Savings Account 5498-SA |

   178→**3.2.3 Intelligent Renaming and Organization**
   179→- **Automatic renaming**: Renames `IMG_20260315_142356.jpg` to `2025_W2_AcmeCorp.pdf`
   180→- **Automatic filing**: Automatically organizes into folders by document type, year, and client
   181→- **Version control**: New versions of the same document automatically overwrite old versions, with history retained
   182→- **Duplicate detection**: Detects and flags duplicate document uploads

   184→### 3.3 AI Data Extraction Engine
   185→
   186→#### Function Description
   187→Automatically extracts structured data from tax documents for import into tax software.
   188→
   189→#### Detailed Feature Points
   190→
   191→**3.3.1 Multi-Format Processing**
   192→- **PDF**: Native PDF, scanned PDF, encrypted PDF
   193→- **Images**: JPEG, PNG, TIFF, HEIC (mobile photos)
   194→- **Handwriting recognition**: Recognizes handwritten notes and signatures
   195→- **Excel**: Extracts data from Excel spreadsheets
   196→- **Low-quality scans**: Enhances low-resolution, skewed, and blurry scans
   197→
   198→**3.3.2 Field-Level Extraction**
   199→Each document type has predefined extraction fields:
   200→
   201→**W-2 Extraction Fields**:
   202→- Employer EIN, Employer Name, Employer Address
   203→- Employee SSN, Employee Name, Employee Address
   204→- Box 1: Wages, tips, other compensation
   205→- Box 2: Federal income tax withheld
   206→- Box 3: Social Security wages
   207→- Box 4: Social Security tax withheld
   208→- Box 5: Medicare wages
   209→- Box 6: Medicare tax withheld
   210→- Box 7: Tip income
   211→- Box 8: Allocated tips
   212→- Box 10: Dependent care benefits
   213→- Box 12: Code + Amount (multiple lines)
   214→- Box 13: Retirement plan/Third-party sick pay/Statutory employee
   215→- Box 14: Other (state disability insurance, etc.)
   216→- State information: State name, State EIN, State wages, State tax withheld
   217→
   218→**1099 Series Extraction Fields**:
   219→- 1099-NEC: Payer information, Recipient information, Nonemployee compensation
   220→- 1099-INT: Interest income, Early withdrawal penalty, Investment expenses
   221→- 1099-DIV: Ordinary dividends, Qualified dividends, Capital gain distributions
   222→- 1099-B: Transaction date, Cost basis, Sales proceeds
   223→- 1099-R: Gross distribution, Taxable amount, Federal tax withheld, Code
   224→
   225→**K-1 Extraction Fields** (supports 1065, 1120S, 1041):
   226→- Entity information: EIN, Name, Address, Type
   227→- Partner/Shareholder information: SSN/EIN, Name, Address, Ownership percentage
   228→- Detailed allocations of income/deduction/credit sections
   229→- Self-employment income, Net rental income, Foreign taxes, etc.
   230→
   231→**3.3.3 Accuracy and Confidence**
   232→- **Target accuracy rate**: 95%+ (MVP stage)
   233→- **Confidence scoring**: Each extracted field includes a 0-100% confidence score
   234→- **Low confidence flagging**: Fields with <90% confidence flagged for manual review
   235→- **Continuous learning**: Model automatically improves after manual corrections
   236→
   237→**3.3.4 Validation and Verification**
   238→- **Cross-document validation**: Consistency checks between W-2 wages and 1099 income
   239→- **Historical comparison**: Compares with prior year data, flags significant changes
   240→- **Logic validation**: Checks if data conforms to tax law logic (e.g., donations not exceeding 60% of AGI)
   241→- **Completeness checks**: Flags missing required documents
