     1→     1→# TAXDOX AI — Complete Product Specification & Market Research Document
     2→     2→
     3→     3→## Executive Summary
     4→     4→
     5→     5→TaxDox AI is an end-to-end tax document intelligence platform built for accounting firms, with its core focus on solving the industry's most painful problem: **PBC (Prepared by Client) document management**. There are document processing tools on the market (Magnetic, Black Ore), and there are PBC management tools (Suralink, Byron), but **no product seamlessly integrates the two** — and this is precisely TaxDox AI's differentiating advantage.

     8→     8→## Part One: In-Depth Market Research
     9→     9→
    10→    10→### 1.1 Industry Background: The Accounting Industry's Talent Crisis
    11→    11→
    12→    12→The accounting industry is facing an unprecedented talent shortage. Since 2021, **300,000 accountants have left the industry**. CPA candidate numbers have been declining consistently, and firms cannot find replacement talent. Meanwhile, across the United States, **80 million individual tax returns are filed through professionals each year**, representing a **$2 billion outsourcing market**, with individual CPA firms paying **$100,000 to $1 million annually** for this service.
    13→    13→
    14→    14→> "Accountants don't have a demand problem; they have a capacity problem." — Blaze O'Byrne, Co-founder of Byron
    15→    15→
    16→    16→### 1.2 Core Pain Point: The Chaos of PBC Document Management
    17→    17→
    18→    18→**Real-world case**: The tax team at JDS Professional Group uses a client portal to send and receive files, but the portal **does not allow preparers to organize received information in a structured way**. Clients send files through various channels including email, USB drives, and even CDs. In large-scale business entities where clients send **over 100 documents**, this lack of organization quickly becomes overwhelming.
    19→    19→
    20→    20→> "Clients give us files, but we can't confirm whether this is what we need. It's like a digital shoebox." — Peter Ordelheide, Tax Manager at JDS

    39→    39→### 1.4 Market Gap Analysis
    40→    40→
    41→    41→#### Gap 1: Missing Intelligent PBC Layer
    42→    42→
    43→    43→Existing PBC management tools (Suralink, AuditDashboard) can only **organize and track** document requests but cannot **understand** document content. AI extraction tools (Magnetic, Black Ore) can extract data but lack PBC workflow management.
    44→    44→
    45→    45→**How TaxDox AI fills the gap**: Automatically generate a **data extraction template** for each request item when the PBC request is sent; immediately perform **intelligent validation** upon document upload (checking completeness, correct formatting, and matching the request); and **automatically map** extracted data to corresponding fields in tax software.
    46→    46→
    47→    47→#### Gap 2: Affordability Gap for Small and Mid-sized Firms
    48→    48→
    49→    49→Black Ore serves Top 100 firms with extremely high pricing. Magnetic and Byron are mid-priced but feature-incomplete. Small firms (1-10 accountants) cannot afford expensive end-to-end solutions.
    50→    50→
    51→    51→**How TaxDox AI fills the gap**: Adopts a **pay-as-you-go** model with low starting prices that scale with growth. The MVP can be built using low-code/AI tools within 30-90 days.
    52→    52→
    53→    53→#### Gap 3: Missing Cross-Border Tax Support
    54→    54→
    55→    55→Existing solutions primarily focus on the U.S. tax system. With **over 190 countries** having different tax systems, multinational enterprises and international individuals urgently need a platform capable of processing tax documents from multiple countries.
    56→    56→
    57→    57→**How TaxDox AI fills the gap**: Builds a **configurable tax rules engine** supporting tax system plugins for multiple countries. MVP supports the U.S., UK, Canada, India, and Australia, with expansion capabilities for the future.
    58→    58→
    59→    59→#### Gap 4: Client Experience Gap
    60→    60→
    61→    61→Existing tools require clients to learn complex portals. Poor client experience leads to delayed document submissions.
    62→    62→
    63→    63→**How TaxDox AI fills the gap**: A **zero-learning-curve** client interface — clients simply **drag and drop files or send emails**, and the AI automatically classifies, validates, and matches request items.

   127→   127→### 3.1 Intelligent PBC List Generation Module
   128→   128→
   129→   129→#### Function Description

   132→   132→#### Detailed Feature Points
   133→   133→
   134→   134→**3.1.1 Intelligent List Generation**
   135→   135→- **Based on prior year data**: Extracts required document types from the previous year's tax returns
   136→   136→- **Client persona-driven**: Generates different templates based on client type (individual/business/trust/nonprofit)
   137→   137→- **Dynamic adjustment**: Automatically updates request items based on current year's tax law changes
   138→   138→- **Multi-language support**: Supports generating request lists in English, Spanish, Chinese, and other languages
   139→   139→
   140→   140→**3.1.2 List Customization**
   141→   141→- **Drag-and-drop editor**: Tax managers can drag to reorder request items
   142→   142→- **Conditional logic**: If client has rental income, automatically add Schedule E related requests
   143→   143→- **Annotation capability**: Add explanations for each request item (e.g., "Please provide all 2025 1099-INT forms")
   144→   144→- **Priority marking**: Flag documents as urgent/important/regular
   145→   145→
   146→   146→**3.1.3 Automated Distribution**
   147→   147→- **One-click send**: Distribute via multiple channels including email, portal, and SMS
   148→   148→- **Personalized links**: Each client receives a unique upload link
   149→   149→- **Bulk operations**: Send annual PBC lists to all clients simultaneously

   184→   184→### 3.3 AI Data Extraction Engine
   185→   185→
   186→   186→#### Function Description
   187→   187→Automatically extracts structured data from tax documents for import into tax software.
   188→   188→
   189→   189→#### Detailed Feature Points
   190→   190→
   191→   191→**3.3.1 Multi-Format Processing**
   192→   192→- **PDF**: Native PDF, scanned PDF, encrypted PDF
   193→   193→- **Images**: JPEG, PNG, TIFF, HEIC (mobile photos)
   194→   194→- **Handwriting recognition**: Recognizes handwritten notes and signatures
   195→   195→- **Excel**: Extracts data from Excel spreadsheets
   196→   196→- **Low-quality scans**: Enhances low-resolution, skewed, and blurry scans
   197→   197→
   198→   198→**3.3.2 Field-Level Extraction**
   199→   199→Each document type has predefined extraction fields:
   200→   200→
   201→   201→**W-2 Extraction Fields**:
   202→   202→- Employer EIN, Employer Name, Employer Address
   203→   203→- Employee SSN, Employee Name, Employee Address
   204→   204→- Box 1: Wages, tips, other compensation
   205→   205→- Box 2: Federal income tax withheld
   206→   206→- Box 3: Social Security wages
   207→   207→- Box 4: Social Security tax withheld
   208→   208→- Box 5: Medicare wages
   209→   209→- Box 6: Medicare tax withheld
   210→   210→- Box 7: Tip income
   211→   211→- Box 8: Allocated tips
   212→   212→- Box 10: Dependent care benefits
   213→   213→- Box 12: Code + Amount (multiple lines)
   214→   214→- Box 13: Retirement plan/Third-party sick pay/Statutory employee
   215→   215→- Box 14: Other (state disability insurance, etc.)
   216→   216→- State information: State name, State EIN, State wages, State tax withheld
   217→   217→
   218→   218→**1099 Series Extraction Fields**:
   219→   219→- 1099-NEC: Payer information, Recipient information, Nonemployee compensation
   220→   220→- 1099-INT: Interest income, Early withdrawal penalty, Investment expenses
   221→   221→- 1099-DIV: Ordinary dividends, Qualified dividends, Capital gain distributions
   222→   222→- 1099-B: Transaction date, Cost basis, Sales proceeds
   223→   223→- 1099-R: Gross distribution, Taxable amount, Federal tax withheld, Code
   224→   224→
   225→   225→**K-1 Extraction Fields** (supports 1065, 1120S, 1041):
   226→   226→- Entity information: EIN, Name, Address, Type
   227→   227→- Partner/Shareholder information: SSN/EIN, Name, Address, Ownership percentage
   228→   228→- Detailed allocations of income/deduction/credit sections
   229→   229→- Self-employment income, Net rental income, Foreign taxes, etc.
   230→   230→
   231→   231→**3.3.3 Accuracy and Confidence**
   232→   232→- **Target accuracy rate**: 95%+ (MVP stage)
   233→   233→- **Confidence scoring**: Each extracted field includes a 0-100% confidence score
   234→   234→- **Low confidence flagging**: Fields with <90% confidence flagged for manual review
   235→   235→- **Continuous learning**: Model automatically improves after manual corrections
   236→   236→
   237→   237→**3.3.4 Validation and Verification**
   238→   238→- **Cross-document validation**: Consistency checks between W-2 wages and 1099 income
   239→   239→- **Historical comparison**: Compares with prior year data, flags significant changes
   240→   240→- **Logic validation**: Checks if data conforms to tax law logic (e.g., donations not exceeding 60% of AGI)
   241→   241→- **Completeness checks**: Flags missing required documents
